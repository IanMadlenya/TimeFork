Apple Q2 2014 financial results:

(July 22, 2014) Apple's profits up and sales increase.

Even as critics complain the company has lost a step, Apple’s profits keep climbing.

The earnings underperformed Wall Street’s estimate in revenue, though the per-share earnings exceeded the forecast by analysts.

Apple announced quarterly revenue of $58 billion and quarterly net profit of $13.6 billion during the call, or $2.33 of diluted share. Compared to the numbers from an year ago, it's a significant improvement.

  • Sales of the iPhone were up: 35 million units were sold—a third-quarter record—generating $19.75 billion in revenues.
  • Despite a shrinking overall market for PCs, it was also a record quarter for Mac sales with growth of 18 percent from previous year.
  • Revenue from iPad, however, declined by 8 percent from previous year.

    • Tesla Q2 2014 financial results:

    (July 31, 2014) Tesla loses money, but proceeds with Gigafactory plans.

    The electric car company is on track to ramp up its Model S production. It’s hoping to get its Model X out the door by early 2015 (after previous delays), and it invests more in its battery pack production factory. Tesla said it generated $769.4 million in revenue for the second quarter, which was substantially higher than the same quarter in 2013. But, overall, Tesla lost $61.9 million for the quarter.

    Tesla’s plans to deliver more than 100,000 cars by the end of next year. Tesla is trying to ramp up Model S production to 1,000 per week. It’s currently making them at 800 cars per week.

    Facebook Q2 2014 financial results:

    (July 23, 2014) Facebook has significant increase in advertising revenues.

    Daily active users (DAUs) were 829 million on average for June 2014, an increase of 19% from previous year. Mobile DAUs were 654 million on average for June 2014, an increase of 39% from previous year.

  • Revenue for the second quarter of 2014 totaled $2.91 billion, an increase of 61%, compared with $1.81 billion in the second quarter of 2013.
  • Revenue from advertising was $2.68 billion, a 67% increase from the same quarter last year.
  • Payments and other fees revenue was $234 million, a 9% increase from the same quarter last year.
